## Purpose

Make CDP more legible for people who were not present for the origin story.

As other people enter the work, private continuity must not become hidden authority. The repository needs to be the room.

## Failure Mode

**Collaborator entry through private initiation.**

If a new collaborator must already know the story, vocabulary, role map, or current canon state before they can participate, the project creates hidden gatekeeping.

Related failure modes:

- stale maps creating hidden gatekeepers;
- contribution expectations living only in chat;
- pull requests that do not state files read, unresolved concerns, deferred work, or verification;
- culture commitments becoming vibes rather than process;
- repair principles being treated as inspirational rather than operational.

## Anti-Pattern Warnings Captured

The contribution path now explicitly warns contributors not to:

- treat eloquence as canon;
- treat confidence as authority;
- treat hierarchy as legitimacy;
- collapse disagreement into delay;
- read quietness as agreement;
- close repair because a form is complete;
- cite theory unless it changes what CDP is obligated to notice, ask, refuse, or repair;
- turn care into control, pity, or paternal management;
- turn repair-efficacy prompts into a scored compliance ritual;
- make the process readable only to insiders;
- use AI summaries as affected-party approval, community authority, or closure evidence.

## Do Not Assume

- Do not assume a new collaborator has read the private chat history.
- Do not assume project culture can be transmitted by tone alone.
- Do not assume a PR is reviewable without files-read and verification context.
- Do not assume a stale map is harmless once other humans are using it.
