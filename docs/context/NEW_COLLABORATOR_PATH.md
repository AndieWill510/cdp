# NEW_COLLABORATOR_PATH — Entering CDP Without Private Initiation

Status: Draft v0.1  
Date: 2026-07-04  
Purpose: Give new CDP collaborators a public-safe path into the work without requiring private origin-story context.

## Why This File Exists

As CDP becomes work that other people can enter, the repository must become the room.

No collaborator should need private chat history, personal initiation, or insider memory to understand:

- what CDP is;
- what is canon;
- what is draft;
- what can be challenged;
- how to contribute;
- what must not be assumed.

Private context may explain how a thing began. It must not be required to participate with integrity.

## What You Should Understand Before Contributing

Before proposing changes, you should be able to answer:

- What is the difference between a proposal, a decision, and execution?
- What makes a decision legitimate in CDP?
- What is the role of Challenge?
- What is the difference between canon, draft, session record, and conversation?
- What file or RFC are you actually changing?
- What downstream files may drift if your change lands?
- What uncertainty remains?

You do not need to know everything. You do need to know what you do not know.

## Canon Map

The controlling map of the RFC corpus is:

```text
rfc/RFC-CDP-000-Series-Index.md
```

If that map is stale, name the map drift.

Do not silently rely on a private understanding of the current state.

## Collaboration Model

CDP collaboration is a council model, not a pyramid.

Roles may include:

- originator;
- challenger;
- coherence reviewer;
- adjudicator;
- recorder;
- canon promoter.

A person or AI system may hold different roles at different moments.

No role confers infallibility.

No role turns fluency into canon.

No role lets dissent disappear.

## What Counts as a Good First Contribution

Good first contributions include:

- fixing broken links;
- making stale status visible;
- improving orientation paths;
- clarifying a glossary term without changing protocol semantics;
- adding missing cross-references;
- naming deferred work that is already implied by the record;
- improving diagrams or examples without overclaiming;
- finding schema/prose drift and opening a challenge.

Avoid beginning with large RFC rewrites unless you have read the relevant corpus and can state the failure mode precisely.

## How To Open a Pull Request

Use the pull request template.

At minimum, state:

- what you read;
- what you changed;
- why the change is needed;
- what status the changed artifact has;
- what remains unresolved;
- how you verified the result.

If your PR touches repair, standing, appeal, affected-party review, sovereignty, consent, or AI collaboration boundaries, be especially explicit about assumptions and limits.

## Challenge Path

Challenge is a contribution.

A challenge should name:

- the artifact or claim being challenged;
- the failure mode;
- the evidence or file basis;
- whether the issue blocks promotion or should be deferred;
- the narrowest repair path.

Do not use challenge as a vibe check. Use it as a governance act.

## Anti-Pattern Warnings

Do not treat eloquence as canon.

Do not treat hierarchy as legitimacy.

Do not treat silence as consent.

Do not collapse affected-party review into institutional approval.

Do not treat repair completion as repair efficacy.

Do not cite theory unless it changes what CDP must notice, ask, refuse, or repair.

Do not use AI output as a substitute for affected-party testimony, community authority, or human adjudication.

Do not make the project readable only to people who were present at its origin.

## Where To Ask Questions

Open an issue or PR with the smallest precise question you can name.

Good question shape:

```text
I read X and Y. I think Z may drift from RFC-CDP-___ because ___. Is this a real drift, deferred work, or an intentional distinction?
```

That is better than pretending certainty.

## Short Version

Read the map.

Name your role.

State what you read.

Distinguish canon from draft.

Preserve dissent.

Name uncertainty.

Repair drift.

Do not let process pretend to be legitimacy or repair.
