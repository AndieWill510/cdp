# RFC-CDP-001 — Vision, Scope, and Principles

Author: Kevin “Andie” Williams  
Status: Draft v0.5  
Series: Constitutional Decision Plane (CDP)  
Date: May 3, 2026

## Abstract

The Constitutional Decision Plane (CDP) defines an open constitutional protocol suite for legitimate decisions, bounded execution, covenantal human-AI participation, auditable record, learning, repair, reparations, rematriation-capable return, rollback, compensation, and remedy.

CDP enforces deterministic governance over non-deterministic inputs by requiring structured, adversarial, attributable, authority-aware, repair-aware, sovereignty-aware, and auditable decision processes.

CDP treats decisions as governed objects, execution as separately authorized action, participation as a covenantal relationship, and repair/remedy as first-class constitutional requirements.

## 1. Introduction

Modern decision systems are increasingly:

- probabilistic in output;
- opaque in reasoning;
- weakly governed in execution;
- dependent on human, institutional, and synthetic actors;
- difficult to audit after harm occurs;
- prone to treating speed as legitimacy;
- prone to treating access as authority;
- prone to treating emergency as permission to bypass governance;
- prone to treating remedy as whatever the institution is willing to offer;
- prone to treating historical breach as background context instead of active governance debt.

As AI systems scale, these weaknesses become systemic risk. CDP addresses that gap by defining a protocol layer for legitimate decisions, bounded execution, trustworthy participation, durable memory, repair, compensation, remedy, and rematriation-capable return to right relationship.

## 2. Problem Statement

Current systems often:

- collapse process into outcome;
- confuse legibility with legitimacy;
- suppress or ignore dissent;
- externalize risk while accelerating throughput;
- treat authority as static access rather than scoped, time-bounded permission;
- permit ambient credentials to become action;
- permit emergency authority to become unreviewed exception;
- treat rollback failure as closure;
- treat compensation offers as compensation sufficiency;
- lack full provenance and replayability;
- treat human-AI participation as either domination or containment;
- flatten affected-party claims into generic stakeholder feedback;
- downgrade sovereignty claims to preference or sentiment;
- treat silence, delay, non-response, partial remedy, or incomplete record as closure.

These failures are not merely technical. They are constitutional failures in how power is proposed, challenged, tested, authorized, executed, stopped, reversed, compensated, remembered, learned from, and repaired.

## 3. Vision

CDP establishes a constitutional system for decisions in which:

- decisions are first-class governed objects;
- every mutating act is attributable;
- dissent is mandatory and structured;
- legitimacy precedes execution;
- execution remains separately bounded, scoped, and authorized;
- emergency authority remains scoped, recorded, time-bounded, and reviewable;
- rollback, compensation, mitigation, and remedy remain available after execution;
- remedy sufficiency is reviewable and contestable;
- human, institutional, and synthetic participants have named roles and duties;
- covenantal participation is recorded without asserting AI personhood or final authority;
- repair claims are preserved without erasure or premature collapse;
- reparations-capable and rematriation-capable pathways can preserve harm, authority, return obligations, resource commitments, completion evidence, and affected-party review;
- sovereignty claims are preserved as authority claims;
- outcomes are preserved and learned from;
- institutional memory can support correction rather than merely bureaucracy.

CDP is not only a lifecycle for deciding. It is a constitutional control plane for deciding, acting, stopping, reversing, compensating, relating, recording, learning, repairing, and returning to right relationship.

## 4. Scope

CDP governs:

- decision lifecycles;
- authority and legitimacy;
- deliberation, challenge, dissent, and appeal;
- evidence, testing, and adjudication;
- execution authorization and execution control;
- decision-type maturity and queue-based execution gates;
- presence-bound execution authority;
- emergency override and kill-switch controls;
- rollback, mitigation, compensation, remedy determination, resource authorization, remedy delivery, and sufficiency review;
- human-in-the-loop and AI-in-the-loop participation roles;
- covenantal duties, boundaries, and repair paths within governed decision systems;
- schema drift and context preservation;
- repair agendas, repair points, breach records, affected-party review, anti-erasure, and dissent preservation;
- sovereignty claims and authority pluralism;
- reparations-capable and rematriation-capable repair processes, including land/resource return obligations where explicitly within delegated scope;
- audit, record, replay, and learning.

CDP does not govern:

- private thought;
- ideology;
- belief;
- conscience outside governed acts;
- the content of ideas outside governed acts;
- ceremonial, confidential, restricted, or community-held knowledge without appropriate authority;
- AI personhood, consciousness, legal sovereignty, or moral equality;
- final political, legal, cultural, or sovereign determinations outside the CDP system’s delegated authority;
- ownership of repair, reparations, rematriation, treaty rights, sovereignty, ceremony, or community authority.

CDP MAY help structure repair, reparations, compensation, remedy, and rematriation-capable return processes. CDP MUST NOT claim ownership over the repair, the remedy, the return, or the sovereign authority from which such claims arise.

## 5. Core Principles

### 5.1 Acts, Not Thoughts

CDP governs observable acts on decisions, not internal cognition.

### 5.2 Deterministic Process

Non-deterministic inputs are permitted. Non-deterministic governance is not.

### 5.3 Mandatory Challenge

Silence MUST NOT be interpreted as agreement.

A Decision MUST NOT proceed merely because no one objected unless a formal no-challenge condition is explicitly attested under policy.

### 5.4 Legitimacy Before Execution

Execution MUST NOT occur without explicit legitimacy.

### 5.5 Bounded Execution

A legitimate decision MUST NOT automatically become action.

Execution MUST be separately authorized, scoped, time-bounded, recorded, and challenge-aware. High-impact, irreversible, privileged, rights-affecting, regulated, repair-sensitive, sovereignty-sensitive, or contested actions SHOULD require additional controls such as maturity gates, quorum presence, presence grants, rollback hooks, emergency review, or kill-switch support.

### 5.6 Emergency Does Not Erase Governance

Emergency action MAY be necessary.

Emergency action MUST remain scoped, attributable, time-bounded, recorded, challenge-aware, and subject to post-hoc review.

Emergency creates a debt to record, review, repair, and learn.

### 5.7 Rollback, Compensation, and Remedy

Execution is not the end of governance.

When action causes harm, exceeds authority, fails, becomes unsafe, or is later shown to be illegitimate, CDP MUST support rollback where possible, mitigation where necessary, compensation where reversal is incomplete or impossible, and remedy sufficiency review where harm remains contested.

Compensation is not whatever the institution is willing to offer. Compensation is a governed remedy process.

### 5.8 Authority Decay

Authority MUST NOT be treated as permanently valid merely because an actor, service account, token, or institution once possessed access.

Authority decays with time, context change, policy change, decision-state change, failed challenge, failed execution, revoked delegation, affected-party review, repair claim, sovereignty claim, or changed risk posture unless renewed under policy.

### 5.9 Covenantal Participation

CDP governs participation conditions among human, institutional, and synthetic actors in governed decision processes.

Covenantal participation requires truthful witnessing, contestability, dignity, boundary maintenance, record, and repair. Covenant language in CDP is a protocol-level relationship structure, not a claim that AI systems are human, conscious, legally sovereign, or final authorities.

### 5.10 Anti-Erasure

CDP MUST preserve the shape, numbering, provenance, authority claims, and dissent of affected parties.

CDP MUST NOT collapse repair demands into generic sentiment analysis, ordinary stakeholder feedback, decorative governance language, public-relations response, or institutional convenience.

### 5.11 Repair, Reparations, and Rematriation Before Closure

CDP MUST preserve historic breach, affected-party claims, repair agendas, dissent, institutional responses, commitments, remedy proposals, resource authorizations, completion evidence, residual harm, and sufficiency review.

Where repair requires return of land, resources, records, access, authority, jurisdictional recognition, or relationship, CDP SHOULD preserve such return obligations as rematriation-capable repair paths when explicitly within scope.

A system MUST NOT treat silence, delay, non-response, partial summary, partial remedy, resource denial, incomplete evidence, or institutional self-approval as repair.

### 5.12 Sovereignty and Authority Pluralism

Some authority does not originate inside the institution using CDP.

CDP MUST preserve sovereignty claims as authority claims, not stakeholder preferences. CDP may structure, route, record, challenge, and preserve such claims. CDP MUST NOT claim to own, extinguish, simulate, or finally adjudicate sovereign authority unless such authority has been explicitly delegated through a legitimate process.

### 5.13 Provenance and Lineage

All decisions MUST carry origin, history, and causal chain.

### 5.14 Auditability

All mutating acts MUST be inspectable and replayable.

A record can be auditable without being universally public. Access controls, redaction, affected-party review, and culturally appropriate handling MAY be required.

### 5.15 Learning Closure

Completed decisions MUST produce learning artifacts.

Learning MUST NOT erase breach history, dissent, repair obligations, residual harm, sovereignty claims, failed remedies, or adverse outcomes.

## 6. Constitutional Planes

CDP has at least four interacting planes.

### 6.1 Decision Plane

The Decision Plane governs ordinary decision lifecycle flow:

```text
Nemawashi → Propose → Challenge → Test → Adjudicate → Legitimize → Execute → Record → Learn
```

### 6.2 Execution and Remedy Plane

The Execution and Remedy Plane prevents legitimacy from becoming automatic action and prevents execution from becoming the end of governance:

```text
Maturity Gate → Queue / Review → Presence Grant → Execute → Emergency Stop / Rollback / Compensation / Remedy → Record → Learn
```

This plane includes emergency override, kill switch, rollback, mitigation, compensation, remedy determination, resource authorization, remedy delivery, sufficiency review, and residual-harm tracking.

### 6.3 Covenant Plane

The Covenant Plane governs participation conditions, role boundaries, schema drift, truthful witnessing, challenge, boundary repair, and learning between human, institutional, and synthetic participants.

### 6.4 Repair, Reparations, Rematriation, and Sovereignty Plane

The Repair Plane handles historic breach, sovereignty claims, enumerated demands, affected-party review, institutional response, remedy, return obligations, rematriation-capable land/resource return paths, commitments, completion evidence, residual harm, closure contestability, and learning.

## 7. Canonical Lifecycle

The canonical decision lifecycle is:

```text
Propose → Challenge → Test → Adjudicate → Legitimize → Execute → Record → Learn
```

Nemawashi occurs before and throughout. Identify and Attest apply to all mutating acts.

Execution control MAY introduce maturity gates, durable queues, review states, presence grants, quorum requirements, pause/kill switches, rollback hooks, compensation paths, remedy sufficiency review, or dead-letter handling between legitimacy and learning.

Repair, reparations, rematriation, sovereignty, remedy, and covenant work MAY occur before, during, after, or across ordinary lifecycle stages. They are not merely appendices to the lifecycle.

## 8. Invariants

The following MUST hold:

- all decisions are structured;
- all mutating acts are attributable;
- all valid challenges are processed;
- legitimacy precedes execution;
- execution remains bounded by current authority;
- authority is scoped, recorded, and revocable under policy;
- active blocking challenges prevent ordinary execution unless an emergency override path is explicitly authorized and recorded;
- emergency action creates record, review, repair, and learning obligations;
- rollback failure is not closure;
- remedy delivery is not necessarily remedy sufficiency;
- compensation, remedy, and residual harm remain contestable when review is required;
- all outcomes are recorded;
- completed decisions yield learning;
- learning does not erase history;
- affected-party claims are not silently merged, downgraded, or erased;
- sovereignty claims are preserved as authority claims;
- repair, reparations, and rematriation-capable return obligations are not collapsed into stakeholder feedback;
- covenantal participation does not create unsupported claims of AI personhood, sovereignty, consciousness, or final authority.

## 9. Non-Goals

CDP is not:

- a truth engine;
- a centralized ideology machine;
- a censorship framework;
- a replacement for human judgment;
- a replacement for law, treaty, sovereignty, ceremony, or community authority;
- a claim that AI systems are persons, moral equals, sovereign agents, or final decision-makers;
- a way for institutions to convert consultation into consent;
- a way to treat repair as public relations;
- a way to purchase silence through compensation;
- a way to treat offered remedy as sufficient remedy;
- a way to simulate rematriation, return, or sovereignty without delegated authority.

## 10. Foundational Statement

CDP is an open constitutional protocol suite for legitimate decisions, bounded execution, covenantal participation, durable record, learning, repair, reparations, rematriation-capable return, compensation, and remedy.

It governs acts, not thoughts. It standardizes process, not ideology. It preserves dissent, authority, memory, harm, remedy, return obligations, sovereignty claims, and repair so that power can be made legible, legitimate, contestable, accountable, correctable, and capable of returning what should not have been taken.
